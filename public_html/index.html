EXECUTIVE TALK


Tony Tatasciore
Senior Director CWx Technology Improvement
It is hard to believe as I write this note we are well into the 4th quarter of 2019. I don’t know about you but for me it seems that we’re so consumed and focused on our mission every day that time just flies by. I lead the CernerWorks Malvern Technology Improvement organization and I am happy to share a brief note with you that will admittedly have a mostly Technology Improvement (TI) oriented scope.

The Bangalore and Kolkata TI teams have continued to make tremendous contributions to the work and results of the MLV TI organization. This spans everything from our most basic (and important) processes of Change Management, SOA and Security through providing outstanding technical expertise and services in the areas of SQL DBA, Citrix, OPENLink, HEP, Java & Authentication platforms, tools such as Olympus, AppD, and Splunk, as well as the release planning and upgrade support for our Soarian clients. The role our India colleagues play in these areas and others has become so essential that it is not an exaggeration to say that we could not be successful without you.

The accomplishments you all contributed to in these areas are surely more than can be covered in a brief note but a few recent examples that are highly impactful include: the development and impending rollout of the DBA PAM solution; the transition of our monitoring/alerting from CA to Olympus; the migration from SQL 2008 to SQL 2012/14/16 (still in-progress); and the migration from WIN 2008 to WIN 2012/16 (still in-progress). All these projects deliver exceptional value and help us achieve higher level goals in the areas of security and audit compliance, technical currency, cost savings and operational efficiency & alignment.

The 360 View of Kansas City

Srinivasa, C| Team Lead, CWx BLR Abilities Lab

A clean city filled with greenery all around and we receive a fresh air to respire. Kansas City boasts many beautiful outdoor public sculptures and fountains, it is also called as “City of Fountains”. The most famous fountain in Kansas City is J.C. Nichols Memorial Fountain, it’s beautiful . Quiet tree-lined neighbourhood streets as well as boulevards showcase hundreds of magnificent and unique works of art . Parking is easy, traffic is (usually) light, and the city is a breeze to navigate. Fountains and statues are used in the entire city for decorative purpose. The creative arts scene in Kansas City is thriving - it’s sophisticated and brimming with history and tradition. Had a wonderful experience travelling to KC.
